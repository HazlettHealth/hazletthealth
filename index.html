About

A GP at the forefront
of cannabis medicine

I am a UK-based GP with a portfolio career spanning primary care, out-of-hours services, and specialist medical cannabis practice. My qualifications include MBChB, a Postgraduate Diploma, and MRCGP membership. Before medicine I trained in herbal medicine, a rigorous and clinically grounded programme encompassing quality control, research methodology, and practice, including time in NHS Dermatology at Whipps Cross Hospital.

My broader clinical background includes care coordination within an Early Intervention in Psychosis service, working with 18 to 36 year olds experiencing their first episode of psychosis. That grounding in mental health, alongside training in CBT and trauma-informed approaches, shapes how I think about the patients I see in cannabis medicine, many of whom carry significant complexity.

In medical cannabis practice I have delivered over 20,000 consultations, with particular depth in neurodevelopmental disorders, chronic pain, anxiety, sleep conditions, and complex high-utilisation patients. I work within the current evidence base for efficacy and risk minimisation. Alongside clinical work I train and mentor GPs and pharmacist prescribers entering this field, and contribute regularly to multidisciplinary team discussions on complex cases.

This site is where I write about the research I find compelling, the clinical questions I keep encountering, and the conversations that matter most in cannabis medicine right now. If you are a clinician, researcher, journalist, or organisation working in this space, I am glad to connect.

How to publish on GitHub

  1. Download the file above — it will be called hazletthealth.html
  2. Go to your GitHub repository in your browser
  3. Click on index.html in the file list
  4. Click the pencil icon (Edit) at the top right
  5. Click the three-dot menu ⋯ then Delete file and commit the deletion
  6. Back in the repository, click Add file → Upload files
  7. Drag in your downloaded hazletthealth.html file
  8. Before committing, rename it to index.html in the filename box
  9. Click Commit changes — your site updates within about a minute

Tip: once you are comfortable with GitHub, there is a faster drag-and-drop method. Ask Dr Claude to explain it when you are ready.
